Take a deep breath. Relax your shoulders. Look directly into the camera lens, not at the screen.

Start by introducing yourself. Say your name clearly and confidently. Pause for a beat. Let the viewer settle in.

Now deliver your main point. Keep your sentences short. Speak naturally — like you're talking to one person, not an audience.

Here's a tip: if you lose your place, just pause. Nobody notices a confident pause. They only notice panic.

Remember to vary your tone. Go up when you ask a question. Slow down when you want emphasis. Speed up slightly when you're building energy.

Let's practice a transition. Say something like: "Now here's where it gets interesting..." Then pause for two seconds before continuing.

Good. You're doing great. Keep your eyes on the lens. The text will keep scrolling — trust it.

As you wrap up, slow your pace down. Lower your voice slightly. Give the viewer a clear call to action: what should they do next? Visit a website? Subscribe? Book a call?
